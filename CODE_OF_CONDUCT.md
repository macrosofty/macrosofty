# Code of Conduct

We want Macrosofty's community to feel like a good braai — people around the fire, relaxed, nobody being a dickhead.

## The short version

- **Be kind.** Disagreement is fine; contempt isn't.
- **Be patient.** Not everyone has the same context you do. A lot of our users are newcomers to Linux — that's the whole point of Macrosofty.
- **Be specific.** "This is broken" is less useful than "when I click X, Y happens, and I expected Z."
- **Don't be a door company PR.** We poke fun at commercial operating systems in our marketing, but we don't tolerate bullying individuals or groups.
- **No bigotry, harassment, or doxxing.** Ever. Any of these three gets a permanent ban, no second chance.

## What we enforce

Zero tolerance:
- Racism, homophobia, transphobia, sexism, ableism, or any other "-ism" directed at a person or group
- Harassment, stalking, or threats
- Doxxing or sharing personal info without consent
- Sexual content or advances in project spaces

Warnings then enforcement:
- Condescending or dismissive tone toward newcomers
- Gatekeeping ("if you don't understand X you shouldn't be using Linux")
- Derailing discussions, bad-faith arguing, or sealioning
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project

## How to report

Email `conduct@macrosofty.<tld>` (placeholder; real address set during scaffold). The maintainer reads this. Reports are confidential.

If the person you're reporting *is* the maintainer, open a public issue or escalate to any listed secondary contact. We'll figure out governance as the project grows.

## Scope

This applies to:
- GitHub (issues, PRs, discussions)
- The Macrosofty website
- Any official Macrosofty chat/community space if we ever add one
- When representing Macrosofty externally (conferences, social media)

It does **not** apply to:
- Your personal social media posts, unrelated to Macrosofty
- Forks that aren't claiming the Macrosofty name
